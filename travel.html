What is Camp Ogontz?

Formerly a girls camp, Ogontz, is a 350 acre private rustic resort, nestled deep within the forests of New Hampshire's White Mountains. The camp rests above its own lake and is complete with mountain views, streams, and waterfalls. On property activities include swimming, canoeing, kayaking, lawn games, and woodburning hot tubs. Additionally, the facilities include a provisions store & café, tennis courts, volleyball courts, and a private boat house. As the leaves begin to change, early October is ideal for viewing the New England fall colors, and Ogontz has miles of wooded hiking trails. You'll find many spots to relax with friends and family, whether it's in the lodge, on the lawn, or by the fire. If you want to arrive and stay at Ogontz for the entirety of the weekend, there is plenty to do and ample space to gather with loved ones. Think… sleep-away summer camp!

ACCOMMODATIONS

CAMP OGONTZ - Stay with us in the heart of the action!

Camp Ogontz has an assortment of rustic lodging options with capacity to sleep ~200 guests on-site. The accommodations range in comfort from "glamping cabins" with screened in walls and shared bathrooms (available for ~120 guests) to private rooms with shared bathrooms and complete lakeside four-season homes (available for ~80 guests total). Linens are provided for all on property lodging.

We invite you to throw stereotypical wedding expectations to the wind and embrace a more rustic, camp-y lodging experience in order to accommodate as many guests as possible on the venue!

NEARBY POINTS OF INTEREST

Franconia Notch

Franconia Notch is a highly trafficed and loved area of New England, perfect hiking and outdoor exploration. Check out All Trails to find a hike that works for you and your group.

Littleton, NH

Otherwise known as “The Glad Town”, this adorable New England town is the origin of the storybook character, Pollyanna. It includes coffee shops, local ice cream spots, and fashion boutiques.

Kancamagus Highway

A 35 mile scenic drive along Rt. 112, The Kancamagus Highway is one of the best fall foliage viewing areas in the country. The Byway takes you through the White Mountain National Forest with breathtaking views of the White Mountains, the Swift River, Sabbaday Falls, Lower Falls and Rocky Gorge.

Lincoln, NH

Lincoln is home to Loon ski resort, one of Tony and Karina's favorite places to ski in the Northeast. During warmer months, you can visit Flume Gorge or take a tour to see local wildlife. Lincoln is another small New England town with plenty to see, do, and explore.

The Riverwalk Covered Bridge

This iconic New England bridge runs over the gorgeous Ammonoosuc River. Located one block away from Main Street, this is a charming pit stop in the heart of Littleton, NH.

Polly's Pancake Parlor

This breakfast parlor is one of the most beloved and famous spots in the area! Highly recommended by all sorts of travelers, locals, and visitors familiar with the area.
